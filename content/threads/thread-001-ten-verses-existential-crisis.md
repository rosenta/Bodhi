**Tweet 2**:
1/ "Among all beings, human birth is the rarest. Rarer still is the desire to know who you truly are." (v.2)

You beat 1 in 400 trillion odds to be here. And you're spending it doom-scrolling. Shankara saw this coming.

**Tweet 3**:
2/ "Let them recite scriptures, worship gods, perform rituals — without knowing the Self, liberation is impossible." (v.6)

You can meditate for 10,000 hours and still miss the point. The doing isn't the problem. The not-looking-inward is.

**Tweet 4**:
3/ "Action purifies the mind, but it cannot reveal Reality. Reality is known through inquiry alone." (v.11)

No amount of hustle will show you who you are. Only stopping will. Your therapist would agree.

**Tweet 5**:
4/ "Consciousness alone is real. The world is appearance." (v.20)

Everything you're chasing — the promotion, the relationship, the follower count — is the movie. You are the screen. The screen never changes.

**Tweet 6**:
5/ "The continuous inquiry into one's own true nature — that is called the highest devotion." (v.32)

Forget candles and chanting. The most sacred act? Asking "Who am I?" and refusing to accept a borrowed answer. That's Shankara's definition of devotion.

**Tweet 7**:
6/ A student walks up to the guru and says:

"What is bondage? How did it arise? How can I be free? What is the Self? Please — tell me." (v.51)

These are the questions that keep you up at 3 AM. They're also the only ones worth answering.

**Tweet 8**:
7/ "Five senses chasing five objects — a moth drawn to five flames at once." (v.78)

A moth dies chasing ONE light. You're chasing five — on a screen designed to kill your attention. This was written 1,200 years before the iPhone.

**Tweet 9**:
8/ "Objects are not loved for their own sake but for the sake of the Self." (v.108)

You don't love them. You love how they make you feel about yourself. Every craving is secretly a craving for your own nature. When you see this, everything shifts.

**Tweet 10**:
9/ "It is never born, never dies, never grows, never diminishes. Even when this body is destroyed, the Self is not destroyed — just as the sky within a pot is not destroyed when the pot breaks." (v.136)

When the pot breaks, the sky inside doesn't shatter. Neither will you.

**Tweet 11**:
10/ "With a controlled mind, know this Self directly. Cross this ocean of worldly existence. Abide as Brahman and be fulfilled." (v.138)

The entire path in four lines:
- Be still
- Look within
- See what you are
- Stay there

Most people spend a lifetime avoiding step one.
