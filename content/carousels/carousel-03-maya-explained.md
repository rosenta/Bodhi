## Slide 1 (Cover)
**Text**: You are living inside a simulation. A 1,200-year-old text already told you.
**Visual**: Dark background. A subtle grid or matrix pattern barely visible behind the text -- not "The Matrix" green code, but a quiet structural overlay suggesting hidden architecture. Bold, clean sans-serif typography.

## Slide 2
**Text**: The word Maya gets thrown around a lot. "The world is an illusion." People hear that and think: "So none of this is real? My pain is fake? My relationships are meaningless?" No. That is a misunderstanding. And Shankara is very precise about what he actually means.
**Visual**: The word "ILLUSION" large and crossed out. Below it, smaller: "Let's start over." Dark background, red strikethrough, white replacement text.

## Slide 3
**Text**: Verse 110 defines Maya as the beginningless, unmanifest power that generates the entire world of experience. It is composed of three qualities -- Sattva (clarity), Rajas (activity), Tamas (inertia) -- and can only be detected by its effects, never directly observed. Think of it as the invisible operating system running your reality.
**Visual**: A simple diagram: a central node labeled "Maya" with three branches -- Sattva, Rajas, Tamas -- each in a different muted tone (white, red, dark grey). Clean, non-mystical, almost like a tech diagram.

## Slide 4
**Text**: Maya does NOT mean "the world does not exist." It means the world does not exist THE WAY YOU THINK IT DOES. A mirage in the desert is not "nothing" -- the light and heat are real. But the lake you see is not actually there. Maya is the mirage-generating function of consciousness.
**Visual**: A desert horizon with a shimmering mirage. Photographic or illustrated, but stark and clean. No mystical overlays. The point is: it looks real. That is the whole problem.

## Slide 5
**Text**: Here is a modern parallel. When you dream at night, the dream world feels completely real while you are in it. You run from threats, fall in love, feel pain. The dream is not "nothing." It is an experience generated by your mind that you mistake for an independent reality. Maya is the same mechanism, but operating at the cosmic level, 24/7, including right now.
**Visual**: A bed or pillow in darkness, with a faint dream-world emerging from it -- cityscapes, faces, landscapes, all translucent and layered. Evocative but not fantastical.

## Slide 6
**Text**: Why can't you see Maya directly? Because Maya is not an object -- it is the lens you are looking THROUGH. You cannot see your own eyes without a mirror. You cannot examine the projector while lost in the movie. That is why Shankara says it can only be inferred from its effects: the entire visible universe.
**Visual**: A camera lens or projector lens, slightly out of focus. The viewer is looking through it, not at it. Dark, moody. The visual makes the conceptual point visceral.

## Slide 7
**Text**: The three qualities of Maya shape everything you experience. Sattva (clarity) -- when your mind is calm and you see things as they are. Rajas (restlessness) -- when desire, ambition, and anxiety drive your thoughts. Tamas (inertia) -- when confusion, laziness, and denial fog your perception. Your entire personality is a recipe of these three.
**Visual**: Three horizontal bands of color -- soft white/gold (Sattva), warm red/orange (Rajas), deep grey/black (Tamas) -- stacked vertically. Clean, minimal. Each labeled with the quality and its keyword.

## Slide 8
**Text**: So how do you "beat" Maya? You do not. Maya is not an enemy to defeat -- it is a power to see through. The rope-snake teaching (verse 199) says: the moment you turn on the light and see the rope clearly, the snake you feared simply ceases to exist. It was never there. You do not kill Maya. You outgrow it.
**Visual**: A rope on a dark floor, clearly visible and harmless. No snake. Good lighting. The image should feel like relief -- the "after" of a fear that dissolves.

## Slide 9
**Text**: Here is the practical takeaway. Every time you are completely identified with your thoughts, your body, your emotions, or your story -- that is Maya at work. Not as punishment. Not as deception. As the natural function of a power you have not yet learned to see through. Recognizing it is the beginning of waking up.
**Visual**: A person looking at a phone, fully absorbed -- then the same silhouette pausing, looking up, and becoming aware of the room around them. Split panel or before/after composition.

## Slide 10 (CTA)
**Text**: Where is Maya running your life right now? What feels "obviously real" that might be the mirage? Sit with that for 60 seconds. Then save this and come back to it next time reality feels a little too solid.

## Caption
Maya is the most misunderstood concept in Indian philosophy. "The world is an illusion" sounds like nihilism. It is not.

Shankara's Vivekachudamani (verse 110) defines Maya as the invisible operating system generating your entire experience of reality. Not "nothing is real." More like: "nothing is real THE WAY YOU THINK IT IS."

This carousel breaks it down -- what Maya actually is, what it is not, and how to start seeing through it.
