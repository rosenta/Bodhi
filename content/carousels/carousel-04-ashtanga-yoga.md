## Slide 1 (Cover)
**Text**: Yoga has 8 steps. The world is stuck on step 3.
**Visual**: Dark background. A staircase of 8 steps ascending into soft light. Step 3 is highlighted. Bold sans-serif text. Clean, architectural feel.

## Slide 2
**Text**: Sutra 2.29: Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi. These are the eight limbs of Yoga. Not "eight stretches." Not "eight poses." Eight stages of mastering the one instrument that matters: your mind. Patanjali laid this out over 2,000 years ago. We have been reading page 3 on repeat ever since.
**Visual**: The eight limbs listed vertically like a ladder or spine. Each word in Devanagari and English. Clean typography on dark background. Step 3 (Asana) subtly highlighted in a different color to reinforce the hook.

## Slide 3
**Text**: Step 1: Yama -- your ethical foundation. Non-violence. Truthfulness. Not stealing. Moderation. Non-possessiveness. These are not moral lectures. They are structural requirements. A mind living in deception and cruelty cannot become still. You cannot meditate your way out of being a bad person.
**Visual**: Five small icons representing each Yama (a dove, a mirror, an open palm, a balanced scale, an empty hand). Arranged horizontally. Minimal, dark background.

## Slide 4
**Text**: Step 2: Niyama -- your personal discipline. Cleanliness (body and mind). Contentment (not needing more). Austerity (choosing discomfort for growth). Self-study (examining your patterns). Surrender (admitting you are not in control of everything). Five daily practices that prepare you from the inside.
**Visual**: Same icon style as Slide 3. Five new icons (a drop of water, a still face, a flame, an open book, a bowed figure). Horizontal layout. Dark background.

## Slide 5
**Text**: Step 3: Asana -- posture. Yes, the physical practice. But here is what Patanjali actually says about it: "Asana is a steady, comfortable seat." That is it. One sutra. Out of 196. The entire modern yoga industry is built on 0.5% of the Yoga Sutras. The postures exist to make your body a stable, painless platform for sitting still. Not for Instagram content.
**Visual**: A simple seated figure, cross-legged, still. No fancy pose. No athleticism. Just a person sitting. The visual deliberately undercuts the modern yoga aesthetic. Dark, clean.

## Slide 6
**Text**: Step 4: Pranayama -- breath mastery. Not "deep breathing exercises." Pranayama is the systematic regulation of the life force through the breath. When the breath is erratic, the mind is erratic. When the breath is controlled, the mind follows. This is the bridge between the physical and the mental.
**Visual**: A simple sine wave or breath-rhythm graphic -- smooth, rhythmic, calming. Perhaps overlaid on the seated silhouette. Subtle blue tones. Clean and scientific in feel.

## Slide 7
**Text**: Step 5: Pratyahara -- withdrawing the senses. Imagine unplugging from every notification, every craving, every itch to check your phone. Not suppression -- conscious withdrawal. Like a turtle pulling its limbs into its shell. You do not destroy the senses. You learn to stop being dragged by them. This is the step modern humans need most and practice least.
**Visual**: A turtle with its limbs drawn in. Simple, elegant illustration. Dark background. The image is calm, self-contained, protected.

## Slide 8
**Text**: Steps 6-7-8 are the inner triad. Dharana (concentration) -- fixing your mind on one point. Dhyana (meditation) -- when that focus becomes effortless and unbroken. Samadhi (absorption) -- when the boundary between you and the object of focus dissolves entirely. These three are not separate practices. They are one deepening movement. Concentration ripens into meditation. Meditation ripens into absorption.
**Visual**: Three concentric circles, getting smaller and brighter toward the center. Labels: Dharana (outer), Dhyana (middle), Samadhi (center). Warm light increasing inward. Clean, geometric.

## Slide 9
**Text**: Here is the key insight most people miss. The eight limbs are not a weekend workshop. They are a life architecture. Steps 1-2 build the ethical and emotional foundation. Steps 3-5 train the body, breath, and senses. Steps 6-8 train the mind itself. Skip the foundation and the whole structure collapses. That is why the most flexible person in the yoga studio can still be miserable.
**Visual**: The 8-step staircase from Slide 1, now fully labeled. A dotted line separates "external" (1-5) and "internal" (6-8). Text overlay emphasizes the structural logic. Clean architectural diagram.

Asana -- the physical postures -- gets one sutra out of 196. One. The other 195 deal with ethics, breath, sense withdrawal, concentration, meditation, and the dissolution of the boundary between self and reality.
