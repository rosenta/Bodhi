## Slide 1 (Cover)
**Text**: You are buried under 5 layers. And you built every one of them.
**Visual**: Dark background, silhouette of a human figure with five translucent layers radiating outward like nesting shells. Minimal, moody, no glowing chakras. Typography: bold sans-serif, off-white text.

## Slide 2
**Text**: The Vivekachudamani says you are not your body. Not your breath. Not your mind. Not your intellect. Not even your deepest sense of peace. You are the awareness underneath ALL of it. Shankara calls these 5 coverings the Pancha Kosha -- the five sheaths.
**Visual**: Simple diagram of five concentric circles, each labeled with a number (1-5). Clean line art on dark background. Label at bottom: "Pancha Kosha -- the Five Sheaths."

## Slide 3
**Text**: Layer 1: Annamaya Kosha -- the food sheath. This is your physical body. The thing you feed, dress, photograph, and lose sleep over. It is made of food and will return to food. You are not this.
**Visual**: Outline of a human body with subtle texture of organic material (earth, grain). Muted gold and charcoal palette. Text in clean sans-serif.

## Slide 4
**Text**: Layer 2: Pranamaya Kosha -- the energy sheath. Your breath, your vitality, the thing that makes the difference between a living body and a corpse. It powers you, but it is not you. When you fall into deep sleep, it keeps running. You disappear. Who left?
**Visual**: Same silhouette with flowing lines suggesting breath and movement. Subtle blue tones. Minimal animation direction if used as motion graphic.

## Slide 5
**Text**: Layer 3: Manomaya Kosha -- the mental sheath. Your thoughts. Your emotions. Your 3 AM anxiety spiral. Your reactions on autopilot. This is the layer most people think IS them. It is not. It is weather. You are the sky.
**Visual**: Silhouette with chaotic, overlapping thought-bubble patterns inside. Slightly more visual noise. Grey and white static texture. Text remains clean and anchored.

## Slide 6
**Text**: Layer 4: Vijnanamaya Kosha -- the intellect sheath. Your decision-making faculty. Your beliefs. Your identity narrative: "I am a designer," "I am a good person," "I am broken." Smarter than the mind, but still a layer. Still not you.
**Visual**: Geometric patterns inside the silhouette -- grids, structures, decision trees. Sharper lines. Cool grey-blue palette.

## Slide 7
**Text**: Layer 5: Anandamaya Kosha -- the bliss sheath. This is the one that tricks the best meditators. That deep peace you feel in meditation? That is not enlightenment. It is the last, most seductive layer -- the closest to the real you, but still a covering. Bliss comes and goes. You do not.
**Visual**: Soft, warm glow inside the silhouette, almost golden. The most beautiful slide. But a thin, visible boundary line separates it from a deeper center.

## Slide 8
**Text**: So who are you? Shankara says: you are the Atman -- pure awareness -- the witness of all five layers. You do not eat, breathe, think, decide, or experience bliss. You are the one WATCHING all of that happen. Unchanging. Unborn. Untouched.
**Visual**: The five layers peel away, revealing an empty luminous center. No figure, no shape -- just open space. Dark background with a single point of soft light. This is the visual climax.

## Slide 9
**Text**: Why does this matter? Because every time you suffer, you are identifying with a layer instead of what is underneath it. "I am anxious" -- that is Layer 3. "I am getting old" -- that is Layer 1. "I am lost" -- that is Layer 4. You are none of these. And the moment you see that clearly, the grip loosens.
**Visual**: Text-heavy slide with the five layers listed as a checklist, each crossed out. Simple, direct. Red strike-throughs on dark background.
