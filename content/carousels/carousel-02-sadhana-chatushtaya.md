## Slide 1 (Cover)
**Text**: Self-discovery has prerequisites. Most people skip all 4.
**Visual**: Dark background. The number "4" large and centered, with four subtle lines extending outward like compass points. Bold sans-serif text. Clean, slightly ominous.

## Slide 2
**Text**: Before Shankara teaches you ANYTHING about who you are, he checks if you are ready. Verse 20 of the Vivekachudamani lays out four qualifications -- Sadhana Chatushtaya -- the four prerequisites for self-knowledge. Think of them as the admission requirements for the most important course of your life.
**Visual**: A closed door with soft light beneath it. Minimal, dark. Text overlaid cleanly. Visual metaphor: the door opens only when these four conditions are met.

## Slide 3
**Text**: Prerequisite 1: Viveka -- Discrimination. The ability to tell the difference between what is real and what is temporary. Between the screen and the movie. Between who you truly are and who you have been pretending to be. Without this, every spiritual experience is just entertainment.
**Visual**: Two overlapping circles, one solid (labeled "Real") and one translucent/fading (labeled "Passing"). Clean Venn diagram aesthetic. Dark background with subtle gold and grey tones.

## Slide 4
**Text**: How to build Viveka: Every time you catch yourself saying "I AM this" -- pause. "I am anxious." Really? Or is anxiety something you are experiencing? "I am my job." Really? Or is that a role you play? The gap between "I am" and "I experience" is where Viveka lives.
**Visual**: Text-focused slide. "I am anxious" with a line through it, replaced by "I notice anxiety." Clean typography exercise. Dark background, white text, red edit marks.

## Slide 5
**Text**: Prerequisite 2: Vairagya -- Dispassion. Not giving things up. Not becoming cold. Vairagya is the quiet power of not being OWNED by what you desire. You can enjoy a meal without needing it to be the best meal. You can scroll without needing the next dopamine hit. Freedom is not the absence of desire -- it is desire without a leash.
**Visual**: An open hand releasing something small (a feather, a spark). Not clutching, not pushing away. Neutral, calm image. Dark background with warm amber highlights.

## Slide 6
**Text**: How to build Vairagya: Pick your strongest craving today -- sugar, validation, your phone. Do not eliminate it. Just delay it by 30 minutes. That pause is not suppression. It is evidence that the craving does not own you. Repeat daily. Watch the grip weaken.
**Visual**: A timer or hourglass icon, minimal. "30 minutes" centered below it. The visual is deliberately simple -- the practice is the point.

## Slide 7
**Text**: Prerequisite 3: Shat-Sampatti -- the six inner disciplines. Shama (mental calm). Dama (sense control). Uparati (withdrawal from distractions). Titiksha (endurance without complaint). Shraddha (trust in the process). Samadhana (single-pointed focus). Six skills. One purpose: making your mind a usable instrument.
**Visual**: Six small icons in a 2x3 grid, each representing one discipline (a still lake, a closed eye, a turned-away figure, a mountain, a flame, a lens). Minimal iconography. Dark background.

## Slide 8
**Text**: Think of it this way: your mind is the only tool you have for self-discovery. If the tool is shaking, distracted, reactive, and untrained, it cannot do precise work. You would not perform surgery with trembling hands. These six disciplines are how you steady the instrument.
**Visual**: A single sharp blade or scalpel, perfectly still. Clean, precise, surgical. Dark steel on dark background. The image should feel calm and exact.

## Slide 9
**Text**: Prerequisite 4: Mumukshutva -- Burning desire for liberation. Not casual curiosity. Not "that sounds interesting." Mumukshutva is the point where your need to understand who you are becomes more urgent than your need for comfort, status, or approval. It is the one desire that extinguishes all other desires by fulfilling their root.
**Visual**: A single flame, not decorative or mystical -- more like a match just struck in the dark. Raw, small, intense. Text overlaid in bold.

## Slide 10 (CTA)
**Text**: Which of the four is your weakest? Be honest. Discrimination. Dispassion. Inner discipline. Or the burning desire for truth. Drop it in the comments. That is where your work starts.

## Caption
Shankara does not hand out enlightenment. He checks your readiness first.

Verse 20 of the Vivekachudamani names four prerequisites for self-knowledge -- Sadhana Chatushtaya. Most people jump to the "who am I" question without building the foundation to hold the answer.

1. Viveka -- knowing real from unreal
2. Vairagya -- not being owned by desire
3. Shat-Sampatti -- six inner disciplines that steady the mind
4. Mumukshutva -- a burning need for truth

Which one is your weakest right now? That is your starting point. Drop it below.
